Great architecture isn't the one with the most boxes on the diagram — it's the one
a team can build, operate, and evolve. Here are a few principles I keep coming back to.

## 1. Optimize for the team, not the slide

The best design accounts for the skills, constraints, and velocity of the people
who'll own it. Match the architecture to the team.

## 2. Make the secure path the easy path

Security shouldn't be a bolt-on. Bake identity, secrets management, and least
privilege into the defaults so doing the right thing is also the convenient thing.

## 3. Start small, prove value, then scale

A focused proof-of-concept that ships beats a perfect platform that never does.
Earn the right to add complexity.

That's the short version — I'll dig into each of these in future posts.
